Chemicals in Agriculture

Balancing productivity with environmental safety

About

Chemicals such as fertilizers and pesticides play a crucial role in modern agriculture by improving crop yields and protecting plants. However, they must be used responsibly to avoid harming soil, water, and human health.

Fertilizers

Nitrogen Fertilizers

Boost plant growth and leaf development. Common types include urea and ammonium nitrate.

Phosphorus Fertilizers

Essential for root development and energy transfer. Examples: superphosphate, DAP.

Potassium Fertilizers

Improve drought resistance and fruit quality. Examples: Muriate of Potash (MOP).

Pesticides

Insecticides

Protect crops from insect damage. Example: Neem-based sprays and synthetic pyrethroids.

Herbicides

Control unwanted weeds that compete with crops. Example: Glyphosate, Atrazine.

Fungicides

Prevent fungal diseases such as rusts and blights. Example: Mancozeb, Copper-based sprays.
